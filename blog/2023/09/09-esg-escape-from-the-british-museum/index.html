ESG and the Art of Resonance: The Escape from the British Museum

September 9, 2023

2022  ·  esg   thrive   · 

"逃出大英博物馆"这一现象级短剧引发了广泛的共鸣和赞誉。
"Escape from the British Museum," a phenomenal short drama from China, has ignited widespread resonance and acclaim.
如果你还没有看过这个短剧，那我简单说一下其情节。它以拟人的方式讲述了一盏收藏于大英博物馆的“中华缠枝纹薄胎玉壶”化成少女，逃出博物馆。路上，她意外偶遇来自故土的亲人，一位中国记者，并最终在他的协助下踏上寻乡之行。当她回到中国，在国内各大博物馆奔走，掏出一叠手写在英文报纸上的毛笔字家书一一诉说着文物们跨越万里的思乡之情、被夺之痛。影片结尾，完成任务的“小玉壶”最终又回到了大英博物馆，一句“中国人不做偷鸡摸狗之事，我们会风风光光、堂堂正正地回家”，让观者肩上又负起了“吾辈当自强”的使命感。
If you haven't watched it yet, let me briefly describe its plot. It personifies a precious "Chinese Twisted Branch Pattern Celadon Vase" displayed at the British Museum, transforming it into a young girl who escapes from the museum. Along the way, she unexpectedly encounters a fellow countryman, a Chinese journalist, and embarks on a journey of rediscovery with his assistance. Upon returning to China, she visits major museums, revealing handwritten letters in English newspapers that convey the homesickness and pain of the artifacts, which were taken from their homeland. The film ends with the accomplished "little vase" returning to the British Museum, leaving viewers with a sense of duty to uphold China's integrity: "Chinese people do not engage in clandestine activities; we will return home with honor."

[Trailer] Escape from the British Museum - Chinese web-film 2023 《逃出大英博物馆》预告片

“文物归家”的主题在中国观众中引发极大共鸣。“玉壶”带回的一封封“家书”，字字泣诉戳中人们心中痛苦的回忆，思乡而不得归乡的遗憾，引发3亿的浏览量，激起一轮又一轮的“逃出大英博物馆”番外，让年轻人再一次对我们自己的历史、文化产生了兴趣。
The theme of "Repatriating Cultural Relics" has struck a deep chord among Chinese audiences. The letters brought back by the "vase" eloquently express the painful memories, yearning for home, and regrets of not being able to return, garnering a staggering 300 million views. This sparked a series of spin-offs and reignited the interest of young people in our own history and culture.

百余年前，我们中国积贫积弱，而世界盛行“强盗法则”—— 1840年鸦片战争以后，超过1000万件中国文物流失到欧美、日本和东南亚等国家及地区，其中国家一、二级文物达100余万件。在全球47国，200多家博物馆里，存放的中国文物至少有164万件。而大英博物馆收藏的中国文物多达23000多件，长期陈列的有2000余件，是收藏中国流失文物最多的博物馆之一。不仅是中国，很多其他国家/地区的文物也遭受了被强权掳走的惨痛历史，比如埃及、土耳其。
Over a century ago, China was impoverished and weak, while the world embraced the "law of the jungle." After the Opium Wars in 1840, over 10 million Chinese cultural relics were lost to countries such as Europe, the United States, Japan, and Southeast Asia, with more than a million of them being first and second-tier relics. In 47 countries and over 200 museums worldwide, there are at least 1.64 million Chinese cultural relics. The British Museum alone houses over 23,000 Chinese artifacts, with more than 2,000 on long-term display, making it one of the largest collectors of lost Chinese relics. It's not just China; many other countries and regions have painful histories of having their cultural treasures forcibly taken, such as Egypt and Turkey.

“文物归家”的主题在中国观众中引发极大共鸣。“玉壶”带回的一封封“家书”，字字泣诉戳中人们心中痛苦的回忆，思乡而不得归乡的遗憾，引发3亿的浏览量，激起一轮又一轮的“逃出大英博物馆”番外，让年轻人再一次对我们自己的历史、文化产生了兴趣。
The theme of "Repatriating Cultural Relics" has struck a deep chord among Chinese audiences. The letters brought back by the "vase" eloquently express the painful memories, yearning for home, and regrets of not being able to return, garnering a staggering 300 million views. This sparked a series of spin-offs and reignited the interest of young people in our own history and culture.

祖国的珍宝文物，却需远赴他国才得窥其芳容， 同根文脉的艺术，如今却被异域之语笼罩。
Our Nation's Priceless Cultural Treasures, Seen Abroad Yet Distant. Art with the same roots, echoing our shared history and identity, now carries the weight of foreign tongues.

显然，"逃出大英博物馆"是一个有着“社会责任感”的创意视频！
作为一直在关注ESG的我，这部视频所采用的“ESG概念”也是其独特之处，ESG代表着环境、社会和治理，强调企业和个人在社会中的责任。
这部爆火的短片提醒着观众，文化保护和回归，应该得到更多人的支持和参与。
Clearly, "Escape from the British Museum" is a creative video that carries a profound sense of social responsibility! As someone who has been keeping a close eye on ESG, I must highlight the unique aspect of this video—the incorporation of the "ESG concept." ESG stands for Environment, Social, and Governance, emphasizing the responsibilities of both businesses and individuals within society. This viral short film serves as a reminder to its viewers that cultural preservation and repatriation should garner more support and involvement.

这样一个ESG短片——究竟是什么因素构成了它的成功？
So, what factors contribute to the success of such an ESG short film?
毫无疑问，它成功最大的原因是——它触及了一个当下社会的痛点，即：尽管祖国日益强大，但仍有大量的祖国文化流散民间。这个痛点足够“大”，有足够多的受众能感同身受，换句话说，这个话题会有极大的“公约数”，进而形成现象级的传播IP --- "最大公约数的话题"能与社会中最多的人深刻共振——这是所有内容策划的核心！
Undoubtedly, its greatest success lies in touching on a current societal pain point: despite our homeland's growing strength, a vast amount of our cultural heritage remains scattered abroad. This pain point is significant, with a broad audience that can empathize with it. In other words, this topic has a substantial "common denominator," forming a phenomenal communication IP—topics that resonate deeply with the most people in society are at the core of all content planning!

那你要问了，如何才能找到“具备最大公约数”的话题？
关键词是ESG，社会责任——社会中有哪些痛点？有哪些让你觉得很难受的现象？有哪些价值观你觉得不对？有哪些值得我们做出的改变？系统的运行出了什么问题？有什么我们当下可以行动起来的，从而让我们未来，让我们下一代过得更好的事情？ 这些社会文化伦理规则等方面的重要议题，这些能引发社会思考和改变的议题，才是值得我们去思考，才是社会也会关注的“最大公约数”话题。
这个社会上有太多的人太忙了，他们没有时间去思考这些问题。如果你能去捕捉时代的脉搏，去代替他们去思考当下的困境，众人该如何破局，去探索我们未来走向何处，与国家和民族产生共鸣。 那你一定会受到最多人的关注。
Now you might ask, how do you find topics that have the "greatest common denominator"? The keywords are ESG and social responsibility. What are the societal pain points? What phenomena make you feel uncomfortable? What values do you believe are incorrect? What changes are worth making? What issues exist in the system's operation? What can we do right now to make our future, and our next generation's future, better? These important topics concerning social, cultural, and ethical norms are the ones worth contemplating and are the "greatest common denominator" topics that society will also focus on.
There are too many busy people in society who don't have time to ponder these questions. If you can capture the pulse of the times, think about the current challenges on behalf of others, explore how society can overcome them, and resonate with the nation and its people, you will undoubtedly garner the most attention.

另外，作品本身充满了创造力，采用了浪漫的叙事手法去构建一个虚拟的故事——这是很“中国”的表达方式，李白简简单单几句诗，就能创造一个浪漫至极的平行宇宙——如”应是天仙狂醉，乱把白云揉碎“，又如“云想衣裳花想容，春风拂槛露华浓” —— 我想，编剧一定是受了李白的启发，才让那盏玉壶变成少女的吧。
而正是因为其叙述方式非现实极尽烂漫，编剧可以在其中可以自由地沉浸和表达情感，创造更有感染力的角色与情节。反过来，这种浪漫的有想象力的方式，又激发了观众的创造力，使得他们能够逃离理性，在视频中宣泄自己积攒的情绪。
Furthermore, the work itself is brimming with creativity, using a romantic narrative style to construct a virtual story. This is a very "Chinese" way of expression—just a few lines of poetry by Li Bai can create an extremely romantic parallel universe. For example, "It must be a celestial maiden's intoxication, wildly crushing white clouds," or "Clouds imagine clothing, flowers imagine a countenance; spring winds brush the threshold, dew is rich." I believe the screenwriter must have been inspired by Li Bai to turn that vase into a young girl. Because of this imaginative narrative style, the screenwriter can immerse themselves and express emotions freely, creating more emotionally engaging characters and plotlines. Conversely, this romantic and imaginative style also stimulates the audience's creativity, allowing them to escape rationality and vent their pent-up emotions through the video.

当然，作品本身的美感与质感也是“爆点因素”，Beauty永远都是最重要的要素之一。但它是必要条件，并不是充分条件。要论充分条件，那还得是找到“具备最大公约数”的话题。
Of course, the work's aesthetic and quality are also explosive factors. Beauty is always one of the most important elements (Baby, beauty, beast). However, it's a necessary condition, not a sufficient one. To talk about a sufficient condition, it must be finding topics that have the "greatest common denominator."

赫拉利在《人类简史》中说，人类文明的秘密很可能就在于，我们能**虚构故事**。
承载这故事的文字与影像的力量是非常强大的，它们有时潜移默化有时振聋发聩，无时无刻都在连接我们的情感，改变我们对世界的看法，并逐渐形成文化上的“新”共识，从而推动社会变革。 Yuval Noah Harari in "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" suggests that the secret of human civilization may lie in our ability to construct stories. The power of words and images that carry stories is incredibly strong; they can subtly influence us or resonate loudly. They are constantly connecting our emotions, altering our view of the world, and gradually forming "new" cultural consensus, thereby driving societal change.

下面我们该思考的问题是：当中国的人均收入正逐渐从1万美元上升到3万美元，如果这个趋势是必然的，那这个趋势将引发哪些变化？我们在文化、民族认同、消费观念、生活态度、审美需求等方面，将有哪些新的诉求和共鸣？
替社会发问，向未来要答案，这或许就是我最应该做的事情。 I believe the question we should be pondering now is: as China's per capita income gradually rises from $10,000 to $30,000, if this trend is inevitable, what changes will it trigger? What new demands and resonances will emerge in terms of culture, national identity, consumption attitudes, lifestyle, aesthetic preferences, and more?
Asking questions on behalf of society and seeking answers for the future is perhaps the most important thing I can do."
